A Brief History

Alfred Street Baptist Church traces its origins to 1803, making it one of the oldest Black Baptist congregations in the United States and the first established north of Richmond, Virginia. The church purchased its 313 South Alfred Street property in 1842 and has worshipped continuously on the site since — the original 1855 sanctuary still stands beside the current 1994 complex.

Alfred Street has long been a pillar of African American religious heritage in Alexandria. During the Civil War the building was commandeered by the Union Army and used as a hospital and recruiting station. The congregation was among the first Baptist churches in Virginia to ordain female deacons (1979) and the first to ordain an African American woman minister (1980). In 2003, the church received a Virginia historical marker on its bicentennial.

The Rev. Dr. Howard-John Wesley has served as senior pastor since 2008.
