Travel Suggestions

The reunion takes place in Alexandria, VA (just across the Potomac from Washington, DC) from July 17-19, 2026.

  • The closest airports are Ronald Reagan Washington National (DCA) and Washington Dulles International (IAD).
  • Amtrak and regional trains arrive at Union Station, which connects to the Metro system.
  • The Metro system covers Alexandria and the broader DC area — the King Street – Old Town stop (Yellow/Blue lines) is a short walk from the hotel. Consider a SmarTrip card for public transit.
  • Rideshare services (Uber, Lyft) are widely available throughout the DC area.

If you are driving, the hotel offers a discounted group parking rate (see Hotel Accommodation below). Street parking in Old Town Alexandria fills up quickly during peak hours, and parking in downtown DC for any day trips can be limited and expensive.

Saturday Museum Excursion

On Saturday morning, we will visit the National Museum of African American History & Culture in Washington, DC — approximately 7 miles from the hotel.

  • Admission: Free (timed-entry pass required)
  • Transportation: Charter bus from the hotel

Opened in 2016 on the National Mall, the NMAAHC is the Smithsonian’s flagship museum dedicated to African American history and culture — and the world’s largest of its kind. Its ten stories (five below ground, five above) trace our shared journey thematically: the underground History Galleries move from the era of slavery through the Civil Rights Movement and beyond, while the upper-level Community and Culture galleries celebrate achievement in music, sports, the arts, and family life. The collection spans more than 40,000 objects, with roughly 3,500 on view at any time. Our scheduled visit runs about 10 AM to noon — many guests find themselves wanting to return another time for a fuller experience.
